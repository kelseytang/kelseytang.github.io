Parental Controls

This is a built-in tool on nabi’s kid tablet, for parents to set rules for tablet usage, such as content and screen time.

Project Goals

  1. Add the internet control feature
  2. Restructure information architecture to accommodate future growth

My role in this project

I was the UX/UI designer, responsible for information architecture, UX, UI and prototypes.

Challenge 1
Redesign Without Metrics

According to the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act in the USA, we are not allowed to track kid tablet’s usage . So we had no metrics to reference when examining the old design.

But as kid’s screen addiction was a social issue, and the demands of protecting child’s online safety were raised, I thought we can focus on:

  • How to help parents protect their children MORE EASILY?
  • Do they really need a google-analytics-like tool to review kid’s tablet activities?

Challenge 2
Design for Busy Parents

To portrait the life of modern families in the US, I searched and read papers from research centers, such as Pew Research Center and Kaiser Family foundation.

According to the researches, I found modern parents are very BUSY. They don't have time for a Google-analytics-like tool, all they need is a very fast way to complete all settings.
