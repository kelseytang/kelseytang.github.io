Update Center Redesign

Redesigned the Over-The-Air update tool for nabi kid tablet.
This project aimed to streamline the the OTA flow for non-tech savvy user.

Project Goals

Streamlined the Over-the-Air update flow for nabi tablets to increase the success rate of updates.

My Role in the Project

  • Information architechure
  • User interface
  • Prototype

Root Cause Analysis
Inspect Why the Flow Confused People

Every time we released an update package, the Customer Care team had to spend a lot of time to notify and educate users.

However, with so much effort, our customers seemed still confused.

↑ The old Update Center UI

The 3 frequently asked questions and their root cause:

Frequently asked questions Root cause
How do I know my tablet is up to date? Lack of update status
What should I do to update my tablet? Lack of instruction
The update process went wrong, what should I do? Lack of error handling

Effort prioritization
Make it easy, then make it pretty

The objective was to enhance the success rate of updates and reduce the workload of the Customer Care team. To achieve this, we aimed to eliminate any obstacles faced by the customers. After analyzing the user journey and taking into account customer feedback, we concluded that the ugly UI was not as significant a problem as the notification system and instructions.
